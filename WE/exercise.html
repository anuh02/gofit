BMI Categories

Underweight

Diet: A balanced diet with more calories and protein. Include healthy fats and carbohydrates. Daily calorie intake: 2,000-2,500 calories.

Exercise: Focus on strength and muscle building. Consult a fitness expert for guidance. Daily exercise regimen:

  • Weight training
  • Yoga or Pilates
  • Cardiovascular exercises

Tips: Eat frequent, smaller meals. Consult a nutritionist for a personalized plan.

Normal Weight

Diet: Maintain a balanced diet with various nutrients. Consume a variety of foods. Daily calorie intake: 2,000-2,500 calories.

Exercise: Engage in regular exercise to maintain overall health. Daily exercise regimen:

  • 30 minutes of moderate-intensity exercises
  • Strength training (2-3 times a week)

Tips: Include fruits, vegetables, and whole grains in your diet. Stay active daily.

Overweight

Diet: Control calorie intake and eat healthy foods. Watch portion sizes. Daily calorie intake: 1,800-2,200 calories.

Exercise: Focus on aerobic exercises and weight loss programs. Daily exercise regimen:

  • Cardio workouts (e.g., running, cycling)
  • Strength training (2-3 times a week)

Tips: Reduce sugar and processed food consumption. Regular physical activity is key.

Obese

Diet: A strict and controlled diet plan is crucial. Limit high-calorie foods. Daily calorie intake: 1,500-1,800 calories.

Exercise: Engage in intensive workouts for weight loss and health improvement. Daily exercise regimen:

  • High-intensity interval training (HIIT)
  • Strength training (3-4 times a week)

Tips: Consult a medical professional for guidance. Follow a structured fitness program.
